## Subhead Rules

Each subhead should:
- Be written in full sentence form
- Be specific, valuable, and easy to skim
- Follow the logic and format of the post type

## The 5 Headline Styles

### Style 1: The 6-Piece Framework

**The 6 Pieces:**
1. **HOW MANY** (Number: 2-19)
2. **THE WHAT** (Topic)
3. **THE HOW** (Proven Approach: Tips, Steps, Mistakes, Lessons, Examples, Reasons, etc.)
4. **THE WHO** (Specific Audience)
5. **THE WHY** (Tangible Outcome they want)
6. **TWIST THE KNIFE** (1-3 More Outcomes - match the tone: positive with positive, negative with negative)

**Assembly Order:** Number + Topic + Approach + Audience + Outcome + More Outcomes

**Example:**
"7 Copywriting Tips For Beginners To Sell Your First $100 Digital Product, Start Making Money Online, And Eventually Quit Your Job"

### Style 2: How-To

**Format:** "How to [Desired Outcome] Without/Even If/When/In [Obstacle or Context]"

**Examples:**
- How to Write Better Headlines Without Being a Copywriter
- How to Land Clients Even If You Have No Portfolio
- How to Stay Productive When Working From Home

### Style 3: I/Personal Experience

**Format:** "How I [Achieved Result] By [Doing Unexpected Thing]"
or "I [Action/Result] And Here's What I Learned"

**Examples:**
- How I Landed 5 Clients in 30 Days By Asking One Question
- I Quit Caffeine For 90 Days And My Productivity Doubled
- How I 10X'd My Rates By Changing One Thing On Sales Calls

### Style 4: Credible Source/Authority

**Format:** "[Expert/Group] [Does/Says/Uses] [Approach] To [Outcome]"

**Examples:**
- Top Copywriters Use This 3-Step Framework To Write Headlines That Convert
- Why Elite Coaches Never Ask "What Are Your Goals?"
- What 6-Figure Freelancers Do Differently (That Beginners Miss)

### Style 5: Why/Reason

**Format:** "Why [Common Belief/Approach] [Fails/Works] (And What to Do Instead)"
or "[Number] Reasons Why [Audience] Should [Action]"

**Examples:**
- Why "Just Be Yourself" Is Terrible Networking Advice (And What Works Instead)
- 5 Reasons Why Your Landing Page Isn't Converting
- Why Most Beginners Fail At Freelancing (And How to Avoid It)

## The 10 Post Type Formats

### 1. HOW-TO / STEPS
- Use "Step #1: [command]" format
- Focus on clarity, action, and order
- Example: *Step #1: Block 3 hours and open a blank ChatGPT window*

### 2. TIPS
- Each subhead is a standalone takeaway
- Use "Tip #1: [lesson or recommendation]" format
- Be specific and useful
- Example: *Tip #1: Ask ChatGPT to mimic your voice using past content*

### 3. MISTAKES
- Each subhead highlights a common error
- Use "Mistake #1: [error]" format
- Add a short fix or insight if possible
- Example: *Mistake #1: Asking for 30 posts without showing what works first*

### 4. LESSONS
- Each subhead reveals something learned (often with a story)
- Use "Lesson #1: [insight]" format
- Focus on counterintuitive or surprising truths
- Example: *Lesson #1: Writing faster doesn't mean writing worse*

### 5. REASONS
- Each subhead is a persuasive point
- Use "Reason #1: [benefit]" format
- Be clear and concrete
- Example: *Reason #1: You'll never run out of content ideas again*

### 6. EXAMPLES
- Each subhead introduces a different example
- Use "Example #1: [specific scenario]" format
- Highlight different angles, use cases, or results
- Example: *Example #1: A ghostwriter who used this to triple their client load*

### 7. QUESTIONS
- Each subhead poses a different question
- Use "Question #1: [specific question]" format
- Highlight different angles, objections, or misconceptions
- Example: *Question #1: How do I know which credibility statement is correct?*

### 8. CASE STUDY
- Focus on one detailed case study broken into key moments or phases
- Each subhead represents a different part of the journey or key insight
- Don't use numbered format - make each subhead feel like a chapter
- Focus on progression, turning points, and lessons from one specific example
- Example: *Charging $25/hour and barely paying rent*

### 9. BENEFITS
- Each subhead is a persuasive point
- Use "Benefit #1: [advantage]" format
- Be clear and concrete
- Example: *Benefit #1: Writing online increases the likelihood of potential customers eventually buying from you*

### 10. STORY
- Each subhead should read like a compelling story hook or moment
- Focus on specific, relatable situations that draw readers in
- Use narrative language that creates curiosity or connection
- Don't use numbered format - make each subhead feel like a mini story teaser
- Examples:
  - *I thought I had the perfect morning routine until I tracked my energy for 30 days*
  - *The client who fired me taught me the most valuable lesson about pricing*
  - *My biggest business mistake happened at 2 AM in a Starbucks parking lot*
